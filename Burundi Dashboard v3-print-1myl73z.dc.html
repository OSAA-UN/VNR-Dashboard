PND 2018–2027 — The Joint Planning Anchor

The revised national plan serves the SDGs, Agenda 2063 and Vision 2040–2060 at once — sector platforms are built to deliver against all of them simultaneously.

The same logic drives institutional innovation elsewhere: dedicated finance institutions (FIGA guarantee fund, BIJE youth bank, BIDF women's bank) and program-based budgeting were introduced to move from sectoral, uniform interventions toward integrated, territorialized public policy — the VNR's stated condition for balanced delivery of both agendas. Ex.: Multisectoral Food Security & Nutrition Platform, explicitly mandated to deliver against Vision 2040–2060, the PND, the SDGs, Agenda 2063 and EAC Vision 2050. — VNR 2026, p.22, 29, 85–86

Q3 · Policy Responses in Motion

Six named, costed and dated instruments
Pacte National pour l'Énergie ("Energy Compact")
National Energy Compact

Mobilizes $3.49B public + private under the revised 2024 electricity law, anchored on the Kabu 16, Ruzibazi and Jiji-Murembwe hydropower plants and the Mubuga solar plant.

Access 30% by 2027 · 70% by 2030 Generation ×3.5 Clean cooking 40% of households

The 2024 law opens the sector to private participation; plus grid extension and decentralized rural electrification and biogas. Sits atop a full policy stack: a sectoral energy-development strategy, a national renewables strategy and an energy policy letter. — p.51–53
Cadre National Intégré de Financement (CNIF)
Integrated National Financing Framework (INFF)

Tracks $1.507B in development-partner commitments through 2035 — Burundi's vehicle for aligning with the 2025 Sevilla Commitment.

$1.507B tracked Roadmap adopted 2024 Sevilla-aligned

Built through a multi-stakeholder review of financing needs, mobilized sources, M&E and governance; commitments led by decent jobs, industrialization and energy, then health, hunger, education and poverty reduction. Shifts from unpredictable external aid toward diversified, structured financing — a "profoundly green" INFF with an aid-and-investment tracking platform inside the public-finance system. — p.xiii, 15, 120–124
Stratégie nationale de lutte contre la corruption 2025–2030
National Anti-Corruption Strategy

Targets structural impunity (SDG 16) — against a CPI score stuck between 17 and 20/100 since 2021, with 80%+ naming corruption a major issue.

CPI 17–20/100, flat since 2021 Justice decentralized to hill level

Launched under the direct impetus of the Presidency; deployed with a justice-decentralization reform pushing High Courts to commune level, Residence Tribunals to zone level and hill-level mediators (Notables collinaires) — plus virtual court hearings that cut informal interactions and improve case traceability. — p.75–76
Plan Directeur de Digitalisation des Services Publics 2023–2027
Public-Service Digitalization Master Plan (PDDSP)

Rests on two public infrastructure pillars: COMGOV (100+ institutions) and BERNET (14 universities); digitized civil registry, hospital informatization, tax and business registration already live.

Evaluations 2025 / 2028 COMGOV 100+ institutions BERNET 14 universities

COMGOV connects institutions over 75 km of network in Bujumbura and 18 provincial offices; BERNET links universities through 10 aggregation sites for online teaching. Sectoral wins: civil registry (with UNDP), hospital informatization (Enabel), communal taxation and business registration via the ADB platform — with OBR, PAFE and FENACOBU already serving users online. — p.111–112
Projet d'Appui aux Fondations de l'Économie Numérique (PAFEN)
Digital Economy Foundations Support Project

A $92M World Bank project (Sept 2024) expanding high-speed internet and accelerating digital adoption across the administration — in service of Vision 2040–2060.

World Bank $92M Launched Sept 2024 Internet 29.3% & rising

Launched by the Vice-President; strengthens public-resource management. Lands on a fast-moving market: internet penetration 29.3% in Q2 2025 (up 3.3 pts in a single quarter), mobile penetration 71%, and 7.66M connected mobile-money subscriptions. — p.112, 114
Vision Burundi 2040–2060
Long-term national vision

The reference frame for everything above: emerging economy by 2040, developed country by 2060 — carried by program-based budgeting as a results-management tool.

Emerging 2040 · Developed 2060 Program budgeting adopted Capital-markets services created

The FIGA guarantee-and-support fund, PPP promotion, and new capital-markets services are designed to mobilize national savings and diversify the financing base — with modernization of mining, transport and public-service digitalization named as priority reform fronts. — p.86, 120

Q5 · Water as a Peace & Development Enabler — 2026 AU Year of Water

Access improved 82.3% → 86.3% (2017–2024) but rural coverage is slipping; Lake Tanganyika is at once the richest resource and the most disruptive flood risk — the closest either report comes to water-as-peacebuilding.

Transboundary Water Governance

Lake Tanganyika fishing is regulated under the Lake Tanganyika Authority; the 2020 VNR adds Nile Basin Initiative membership as a standing regional framework.

Via the Convention on Sustainable Management of the lake and a regional charter binding member states on fisheries in the lake and its basin. Nationally: biological closure periods, minimum catch sizes and community co-management. — p.72–73; 2020 VNR p.85–86
Water Security as Human Security

Lake Tanganyika's overflows link directly to displacement, health problems, school dropout and water pollution — water treated as a driver of social stability.

The 2020–2024 flood cycle destroyed housing along the lakeshore and in Gatumba; degraded mental health as urban green space disappears. Citizens' 2030 aspiration: a country that actively protects ecosystems, teaches environmental SDGs from primary school, and brings youth into climate decisions — a water-to-human-security linkage in substance, if not in peacebuilding language. — p.70–74
Restoring Water Ecosystems

A visible restoration agenda backs the access numbers: Nyamabuno marsh, Ntahangwa river banks, buffer zones around Lakes Tanganyika and Cohoha.

Plus rehabilitation of the Buterere wastewater-treatment station, watershed and marshland management, and the national reforestation drive "Ewe Burundi Urambaye." The VNR presents these as the resilience layer that makes durable water access possible. — p.61–62, 74
Policy Anchoring & the Rural Gap

Water anchored in a dedicated PND axis, a National Water Policy and Water Strategy — but the binding constraint is territorial.
