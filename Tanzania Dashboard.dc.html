Dira 2050 & SDG Integration

Tanzania does not run a separate tracking tool for the Sustainable Development Goals. The 2030 Agenda is anchored in the Tanzania Development Vision 2050 (Dira 2050), the country's emerging long-term national vision, and delivered through National Five-Year Development Plans (FYDPs). The review is deliberately timed at the transition from the Third Five-Year Development Plan (FYDP III) to the Fourth (FYDP IV) - the first implementation phase of Dira 2050.

National vision - Dira 2050 & FYDP IV
A deliberate hand-off between plans

With four years remaining to 2030, Tanzania timed this review to the transition between FYDP III and FYDP IV - the first implementation phase of Dira 2050 - explicitly designing the new plan to accelerate lagging SDGs.

Rural infrastructure investment is paying off

Tanzania has made strong progress expanding access to water and sanitation services, particularly in rural areas, where access to piped and protected water points reached 85.2% in 2025. This rural-led pattern mirrors the electricity story, where every village now has grid access even as household connection rates lag — infrastructure reach is outpacing full service quality in both sectors.

Infrastructure momentum carries across sectors

The same national investment push that lifted rural water access to 85.2% also delivered universal village-level electricity grid reach and a 4,504.9 MW power system anchored by the Julius Nyerere Hydropower Plant. Sustaining this momentum into sanitation, hygiene and full household-level service quality is the clear next chapter for Tanzania's Year of Water Sustainability agenda.
