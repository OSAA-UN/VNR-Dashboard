GDP growth and inflation, 2020–2025
Survolez les points pour les valeurs et sources

La croissance du PIB a atteint 6,9 % en 2025, avec une perspective d'environ 10 % pour 2026–2027, portée par le développement du minerai de fer de Simandou. Inflation was tamed from 5.1% to 3.1% through prudent monetary policy. These indicators represent the macroeconomic stability the review foregrounds as the foundation for the 2030 Agenda in Guinea.

Sources : RNV 2026, p.52 · Base de données ODD des ONU, indicator 7.1.1

Electricity access is improving (~46% national) and improved water access has reached ~74%, progress driven by hydroelectric investment and municipal water infrastructure. Guinea's mining revenues — from bauxite, the Simandou iron-ore project and gold — are the primary financing vehicle for infrastructure expansion.

Durabilité de la dette et revenus

Guinea's fiscal trajectory is anchored by mining: bauxite revenues provide a stable base and Simandou will transform the outlook from 2026. The challenge is translating resource wealth into durable public goods — infrastructure, education and health — rather than enclave growth.

External debt and fiscal position

External debt has been stabilising as mining revenues grow. The Simandou project brings both private investment and new fiscal capacity. The DISE system tracks revenue against SDG financing needs, ensuring alignment between budget allocation and development priorities.

Guinea as West Africa's water tower

The Fouta Djallon plateau is the source of the Niger, Senegal and Gambia rivers — making Guinea a water-tower state for hundreds of millions downstream. The OMVG (Organisation pour la mise en valeur du fleuve Gambie) manages shared hydroelectric development across Guinea, Senegal, Gambia and Guinea-Bissau.

Sources : RNV 2026, p.72–73 · RNV 2024, p.85–86
Hydro and the clean-energy transition

Guinea's hydroelectric strategy is simultaneously its clean-energy transition and its water-sustainability strategy: the Souapiti dam (450 MW, operational 2021) and the Sambangalou project (OMVG) deliver clean power while regulating river flows. The 26,000 MW hydroelectric potential positions Guinea as a regional energy exporter aligned with the Année de la durabilité de l'eau.

Source : RNV 2026, p.61–62, 74
Water, energy and sovereignty

Guinea's sovereign implementation of the 2030 Agenda connects water and energy as two sides of the same hydroelectric asset. The Fouta Djallon is simultaneously the water tower of West Africa and the hydroelectric base of Guinea's energy sovereignty. The Année de la durabilité de l'eau for Guinea is a year of hydro investment and rural water-access extension.

Source : RNV 2026, p.70–74
