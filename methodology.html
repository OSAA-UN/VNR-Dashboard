Analytical Methodology
& Technical Notes

This document sets out the analytical approach, data sources, structural decisions and limitations underlying the 19 country dashboards produced by OSAA for the 2026 High-Level Political Forum on Sustainable Development.
01

Purpose and Intended Use

The VNR Dashboards were produced using a combination of large language models (LLMs), large-scale structured datasets and machine learning techniques. OSAA trained and prompted LLMs on the full text of each country's VNR document, the UN SDG Database indicator series, and prior VNR submissions, enabling the models to extract thematic priorities, identify cross-cycle continuities, and surface the most frequently referenced policy concepts from each review.

The analytical framework — including the six SDG focus themes, the continuity classification (maintained vs. evolved), and the concept frequency rankings — was generated through iterative LLM inference guided by OSAA subject-matter experts. Each output was reviewed, verified against primary source page references, and corrected where the model diverged from the source text.

The dashboards are synthesis and analysis tools, not official UN statistical products. All quantitative figures are drawn from primary sources (VNR documents and the UN SDG Database); OSAA has not altered or reweighted any data point.

Nature of product
Synthesis Dashboards
Seven-tab interactive profiles combining VNR narrative with UN SDG Database indicators and chart visualisations.

02

Country Coverage and Selection

All 19 African countries presenting VNRs at HLPF 2026 are included.

03

Dashboard Structure: Seven Analytical Tabs

Every dashboard uses the same seven-tab structure, ensuring comparability across the cohort. The tab sequence follows the standard VNR reporting logic: macro overview → SDG spotlight → integration framework → policy instruments → financing → fiscal position → water.

# Tab Content and sources
1 Overview & Trends Five headline metric tiles (electricity access, water access, domestic revenue, public debt, country-specific fifth indicator) with interactive trend charts and narrative synthesis. Sourced from VNR + UN SDG Database.
2 2026 Focus Goals The five SDGs each country selected for in-depth review at HLPF 2026. Each goal shows key indicators, an achievement and a forward priority drawn verbatim from the VNR.
3 Joint Implementation How each country aligns the 2030 Agenda with Agenda 2063 through its national planning framework — integration architecture, monitoring system and connectivity indicators.
4 Policy Responses Six national policy instruments named in each VNR as primary SDG delivery vehicles. Each panel shows headline metrics, a narrative description and the SDGs addressed.
5 Financing Three financing pillars (domestic, international/concessional, private/blended) with a bar chart ranking development partner commitments by sector prominence in the VNR.
6 Debt & Revenue Public debt trajectory, internal vs. external debt composition and domestic revenue trend. Sourced from UN SDG Database indicator 17.4.1 and VNR fiscal chapters.
7 Year of Water Sustainability Dedicated to the AU 2026 theme. Covers water access metrics, water stress, IWRM score and country-specific water narrative. Sourced from VNR + WHO/UNICEF JMP + UN SDG Database indicator 6.5.1.
04

Primary Data Sources

Every figure displayed in the dashboards is traceable to one of the following primary sources. Source footnotes appear on each chart and card within the dashboards.

VNR 2026 Voluntary National Review documents — primary source for all narrative content, policy instrument descriptions, national targets and country-reported indicators. Page references are cited where precision matters. Available at hlpf.un.org/countries.
UN SDG DB United Nations SDG Database — internationally harmonised time-series data on electricity access (7.1.1), water access (6.1.1), sanitation (6.2.1), tax revenue (17.1.1), debt service (17.4.1), IWRM (6.5.1) and GDP growth (Goal 17 series). Available at unstats.un.org/sdgs/dataportal.
Prior VNRs Earlier Voluntary National Reviews — used to construct multi-year trend series for countries with prior reviews (e.g. DRC 2020 & 2023, Senegal 2022, Tanzania 2023). Cited by year.
05

Indicator Selection and Analytical Framing

The five headline indicators on the Overview tab were selected to be consistent across the cohort while reflecting each country's most salient development profile. Electricity access and water access are universal; domestic revenue (tax/GDP) and public debt/GDP are the two most commonly tracked fiscal indicators in VNR financing chapters. The fifth tile is country-specific — it captures the indicator each VNR most prominently highlighted.


The six VNR Responses per country are drawn from the policy chapters of each VNR, ranked by prominence (number of pages, depth of treatment, headline placement). OSAA has not evaluated the effectiveness of any policy instrument.

⚠ Limitations on comparability

Cross-country comparability is indicative only. VNRs use different methodologies, base years and data sources. A figure of 72% electricity access in one country and 73% in another does not necessarily reflect a meaningful difference.

Financing figures are illustrative. Development partner commitment bar charts show relative prominence in VNR documentation, not verified disbursement data. Bar lengths represent OSAA's reading of the VNR's own prioritisation, not contracted amounts.

Debt composition charts are indicative. The internal/external debt split uses indicative trajectories between published data points. Countries use different definitions of domestic vs. external debt.

06

African Union Theme 2026: Year of Water Sustainability

The African Union has designated 2026 the Year of Water Sustainability. OSAA added a dedicated seventh tab to each dashboard to reflect this theme — connecting each country's water access and water stress profile to their VNR's water chapter and the AU's continental water agenda.

The water stress indicator (withdrawal as % of available renewable freshwater resources) is sourced from the UN SDG Database (indicator 6.4.2). The IWRM implementation score (indicator 6.5.1) measures the degree of integrated water resource management in place. Both are internationally harmonised and comparable across the cohort.
