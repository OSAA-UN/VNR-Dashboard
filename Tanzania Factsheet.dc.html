Voluntary National Review  ·  2026  ·  High-Level Political Forum
TANZANIA
Accelerating the 2030 Agenda through whole-of-nation PLUS action, guided by Tanzania Development Vision (DIRA) 2050.
This is a draft document prepared for consultation and review. The content is subject to revision and should not be cited or circulated as a final version.
1. Six themes driving Tanzania's review
SDG 6 · Clean Water & Sanitation
6
Water Access & Self-Reliance
Rural water coverage rose to 85% and regional centres to 92%, with non-revenue water and sanitation flagged as priorities for acceleration.
SDG 7 · Affordable & Clean Energy
7
Energy Security as a Growth Engine
The Julius Nyerere Hydropower Project doubled generation capacity and connected all 26 regions; electricity access reached 86% with renewables at 80%.
SDG 9 · Industry, Innovation & Infrastructure
9
Connectivity Revolution
The Standard Gauge Railway cut Dar–Dodoma travel from 12 hours to 3, carrying nearly 5 million passengers, while internet penetration surged from 49% to 85%.
SDG 11 · Sustainable Cities & Communities
11
Reshaping Economic Geography
Expanded ports, aviation and the railway make Tanzania a regional trade and mobility hub, opening new opportunities across East Africa.
SDG 13 · Climate Action
13
Climate Resilience Pathway
Strengthening agriculture, water and urban systems tackles disaster preparedness — one of three acceleration pathways for 2026–2030 under Dira 2050.
SDG 17 · Partnerships for the Goals
17
Fiscal Self-Reliance
Domestic revenue climbed to 16.4% of GDP and budget self-reliance rose from 58% to 78%, reinforcing the financing base for the SDGs.
